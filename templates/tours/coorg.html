About Coorg

Also known as Kodagu, Coorg is a picturesque hill station in Karnataka, famous for its coffee plantations, spice gardens, and breathtaking landscapes. At an altitude of 1,525 meters, it offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness.

Coffee Capital of India

Home to some of India's finest coffee estates and the birthplace of Indian coffee culture

Top Attractions

Coffee Plantations

Sprawling coffee estates offering guided tours and fresh coffee tasting experiences

Abbey Falls

Spectacular waterfall cascading from 70 feet amidst lush coffee and spice plantations

Raja's Seat

Scenic viewpoint offering panoramic views of misty hills and beautiful sunsets

Dubare Elephant Camp

Interactive elephant camp where you can feed, bathe, and learn about these gentle giants

Madikeri Fort

Historic fort built by Muddu Raja in 1681, now housing a museum and palace

Talakaveri

Sacred birthplace of River Kaveri with a beautiful temple and mountain views

Best Time to Visit

Summer (Mar-May)

Pleasant weather perfect for coffee plantation tours and outdoor activities

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

Lush green landscapes and waterfalls at their magnificent best

Winter (Oct-Feb)

Cool and misty weather, ideal for sightseeing and coffee estate walks
