About Kodaikanal

Perched at an altitude of 2,133 meters in the Western Ghats of Tamil Nadu, Kodaikanal is a mesmerizing hill station known for its pristine lakes, misty mountains, and pleasant climate. Often called the "Princess of Hill Stations," it offers a perfect retreat for nature lovers.

Gift of the Forest

Kodaikanal means "Gift of the Forest" in Tamil, established by American missionaries in 1845

Top Attractions

Kodaikanal Lake

Star-shaped artificial lake perfect for boating, cycling, and horse riding around its perimeter

Coaker's Walk

1-kilometer paved path offering spectacular valley views and telescope house

Bryant Park

20.5 acres botanical garden with annual flower shows and diverse plant species

Pillar Rocks

Three giant rock pillars standing 122 meters high with breathtaking valley views

Silver Cascade Falls

180-feet waterfall that appears silver as it cascades down the rocks

Pine Forests

Serene pine plantations perfect for nature walks and photography

Best Time to Visit

Summer (Mar-May)

Peak season with perfect weather for sightseeing and outdoor activities

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

Lush green landscapes, ideal for experiencing natural beauty

Winter (Oct-Feb)

Cool and misty weather, perfect for cozy stays and serene atmosphere
