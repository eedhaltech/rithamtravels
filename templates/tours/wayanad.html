About Wayanad

Nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Wayanad is a pristine hill district known for its lush forests, exotic wildlife, and spice plantations. At an altitude of 700-2100 meters, it offers a perfect escape into nature's lap with its rich biodiversity and tribal heritage.

Biodiversity Hotspot

Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve with rich flora, fauna, and indigenous tribal communities

Top Attractions

Banasura Sagar Dam

India's largest earthen dam with boating facilities and scenic mountain views

Soochipara Falls

Three-tiered waterfall perfect for rock climbing and natural pool swimming

Chembra Peak

Highest peak in Wayanad with heart-shaped lake and breathtaking trekking trails

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Rich wildlife sanctuary home to elephants, tigers, leopards, and exotic birds

Edakkal Caves

Ancient caves with prehistoric petroglyphs dating back to 6000 BC

Spice Plantations

Aromatic spice gardens with cardamom, pepper, vanilla, and coffee plantations

Best Time to Visit

Summer (Mar-May)

Pleasant weather ideal for wildlife spotting and outdoor activities

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

Lush green landscapes and waterfalls at their magnificent best

Winter (Oct-Feb)

Perfect weather for trekking, plantation visits, and wildlife safaris
