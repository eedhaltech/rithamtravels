About Munnar

Nestled in the Western Ghats at 1,600 meters above sea level, Munnar is Kerala's most popular hill station. Famous for its sprawling tea plantations, exotic wildlife, and pleasant climate, it's a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Tea Capital of Kerala

Home to some of the world's highest tea plantations and the famous Tata Tea Museum

Top Attractions

Tea Plantations

Endless rolling hills covered with lush green tea gardens offering scenic walks and photography

Mattupetty Dam

Beautiful concrete arch dam with boating facilities and stunning mountain views

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and the highest peak in South India - Anamudi

Attukal Waterfalls

Cascading waterfalls surrounded by lush greenery, perfect for trekking and nature walks

Top Station

Highest point in Munnar offering panoramic views of Tamil Nadu plains and tea estates

Neelakurinji Blooms

Rare blue flowers that bloom once every 12 years, creating a magical carpet across hills

Best Time to Visit

Summer (Mar-May)

Pleasant weather perfect for sightseeing and tea garden walks

Monsoon (Jun-Sep)

Lush green landscapes and waterfalls at their best, ideal for nature lovers

Winter (Oct-Feb)

Cool and misty mornings, perfect for tea plantation tours and wildlife spotting
